§ 02 — How it works

Two ports, four hops, zero terminal.

DroidProxy boots two local services from the menu bar app: a thin TCP proxy that injects thinking parameters, and a child process (CLIProxyAPIPlus) that handles OAuth and talks to upstream APIs.

01 AI client
Factory.ai Droid, Claude Code, Cursor, or any tool configured with a custom OpenAI-compatible base URL.
sends tolocalhost:8317
02 ThinkingProxy
User-facing TCP proxy. Injects thinking: {type:"adaptive"} + per-model effort, then forwards.
forwards to127.0.0.1:8318
03 CLIProxyAPIPlus
Bundled child process managed by ServerManager. Holds OAuth tokens, refreshes them automatically, signs upstream requests.
resolvesOAuth → signed
04 Upstream provider
Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google — same wire format, your subscription, your rate limits, no middleman API key.
terminates atapi.anthropic.com

The nuclear button for Sonnet 4.6.

One toggle forces classic extended thinking on every Sonnet 4.6 request. DroidProxy rewrites the request body to push thinking budget to its ceiling. Opus 4.7 doesn't need this override — it ships with adaptive thinking on.

§ 04 — Install

From DMG to first request in under a minute.

Releases are code-signed and notarized by Apple. Existing installs auto-update via Sparkle — you'll never have to do this twice.

01

Grab the latest DMG

From github.com/anand-92/droidproxy/releases/latest. Pick DroidProxy-arm64.dmg for Apple Silicon.

Download .dmg
02

Open from the menu bar

The DroidProxy icon lands top-right. Click → Settings to open the configuration window.

03

Sign into Claude, Codex, or Gemini

Each provider opens a real OAuth flow in your browser — no API key handling, no token files to lose. Credentials are monitored and auto-refreshed.

04

Click Apply on Factory custom models

DroidProxy registers seven pre-configured models in your Factory client pointed at localhost:8317. Restart your Droid session — done.
